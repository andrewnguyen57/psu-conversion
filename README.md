# Project Overview
This project convert any standard Desktop ATX Power Supply to a benchtop power supply. The design implement a 3V3, 5V and 12V with a variable voltage module outputs.

---
# Why build this?
Benchtop power supplies are sold readily everywhere on Amazon, Aliexpress, etc. However, they are very expensive.
ATX power supplies generally inexpensive but can output a lot of power. 
For example, the power supply used in this probject can output 500W, it can deliver 18A at 3V3, 18A at 5V, and 40A at 12V, that is trememdously good power output and a comparable off the shelf power supply will be very expensive.


---
# Build process
This is a documentation of my process buidling the project, it is not a full tutorial, follow my steps at your own risks. Knowledge of electrical engineering is highly recommended.
## PSU
![Power Supply](images/cm-500-psu.jpeg)
- For this project, I chose to use a Cooler Master Elite 500W V2, which was a power supply from a retired computer. It still works great and the internal components still have a lot of life left.
- You may choose any power supply you want, make sure you know its design, sizing that fits in the case and output capability.
## Case
![Case](images/case)
- The case I designed specifically to use for my PSU, but it can fit most ATX power supply within a 450-650W range. A large power supply is not recommended since you will most likely not use most of its power output and it will be to large
- The case is designed to have a 3V3, 5V, 12V and variable voltage output using the ZK4KX/ZK5KX module
